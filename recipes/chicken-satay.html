Chicken Satay

Chicken satay is well known in southeast Asia, mostly in Thailand and Malaysia. The satay is a marinade primarily with yellow curry, and the chicken is cooked on a grill as skewers. It is commonly served with a thick, peanut sauce and sold as street food.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb chicken thighs
  • bamboo skewers soaked in cold water for 2 hours
  • marinade powder
  • peanut sauce powder
  • 3/4 cups of coconut milk or water
  • 2 tbsp oil

Steps

  1. Cut the chicken into 1 inch cubes.
  2. Combine the marinade powder with a little bit of water, until it becomes a paste.
  3. Thoroughly coat the chicken in the marinade. Let sit for at least 2 hours, ideally overnight.
  4. Once ready, put 3-4 chicken cubes onto the bamboo skewers.
  5. Grill the skewers for 2-3 minutes on each side, until the meat is cooked and the outside is charred.
  6. Pour the peanut sauce powder into a small saucepan and add the coconut milk and oil. Bring to a boil, then turn the heat down and let simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  7. Serve with rice and cucumbers on the side.
