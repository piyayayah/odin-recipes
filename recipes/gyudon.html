Gyudon

A Japanese dish that translates to beef (gyu) over rice (don). Thinly sliced beef, usually rib eye, marinated in soy sauce, sake, sugar, and ginger. It's usually cooked with sliced onions and served over rice with an egg, pepper flakes, and pickled ginger.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb thinly sliced beef
  • 6 tbsp soy sauce
  • 4 tbsp sake
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tsp grated ginger
  • 600 ml water
  • 1/2 white onion
  • optional: egg, pepper flakes, pickled ginger

Steps

  1. Combine soy sauce, sake, sugar, and grated ginger in a bowl. Mix well.
  2. In a pan, combine the water and onions and bring to a boil.
  3. Once the water is boiling, turn the heat down to medium and put the beef in the pan.
  4. Pour the sauce over the beef and let simmer until the beef is cooked.
  5. Serve over a bowl of rice.
  6. Add a poached egg or a raw egg yolk, pepper flakes, and pickled ginger for garnish.
